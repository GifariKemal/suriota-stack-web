Core Capabilities

Predictive Maintenance

Unplanned downtime costs industrial facilities between $10,000 and $250,000 per hour depending on industry. Reactive maintenance — fixing things after they break — is the most expensive approach. Even preventive maintenance (scheduled replacements) wastes money by replacing healthy components prematurely.

SURIOTA's predictive maintenance solution uses machine learning to forecast equipment failures days or weeks in advance:

  • Vibration Analysis: FFT and time-domain feature extraction from accelerometer data to detect bearing wear, shaft misalignment, and gear tooth defects.
  • Temperature Signature: Thermal trend analysis for motors, transformers, and heat exchangers — identifying insulation degradation and lubrication breakdown.
  • Current Signature Analysis: Motor current waveform analysis (MCSA) to detect rotor bar cracks, bearing defects, and load anomalies without additional sensors.
  • Maintenance Scheduling: AI-optimized work order scheduling that balances failure risk, spare parts availability, and production constraints.

Typical Results: 30-50% reduction in unplanned downtime, 20-40% reduction in maintenance costs, 2-5x return on investment within 12 months.

Anomaly Detection

Not every failure mode has historical examples. Unknown failure modes, process drift, and novel anomalies require unsupervised learning approaches:

  • Unsupervised Anomaly Models: Autoencoders, isolation forests, and LSTM-based anomaly detection that learn "normal" behavior and flag deviations — even for failure modes never seen before.
  • Real-Time Process Monitoring: Continuous monitoring of hundreds of process variables with dynamic thresholds that adapt to seasonal variations and production changes.
  • Root Cause Analysis Support: When anomalies are detected, our system highlights the most correlated variables to guide maintenance teams toward the root cause.

OEE & Production Analytics

Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE) is the gold standard metric for manufacturing productivity. But most companies calculate OEE manually from shift reports — a week after the fact. SURIOTA automates OEE calculation and adds predictive layers:

  • Automated OEE Calculation: Real-time availability, performance, and quality metrics calculated directly from machine data — no manual data entry.
  • Downtime Analysis: Automatic categorization of downtime events (changeover, breakdown, material shortage, operator error) with Pareto analysis.
  • Bottleneck Identification: Machine learning analysis of production flow to identify true bottlenecks vs perceived bottlenecks.
  • Shift Performance Comparison: Data-driven insights into shift-to-shift performance variations and best practice identification.

Energy Analytics

Energy is typically the second-largest operating cost for industrial facilities after labor. SURIOTA's energy analytics solutions uncover hidden optimization opportunities:

  • Consumption Pattern Analysis: Disaggregate energy usage by machine, line, shift, and product to identify the true cost drivers.
  • Peak Demand Forecasting: Predict peak demand events and automatically recommend load shedding or scheduling changes to avoid demand charges.
  • Cost Optimization: Multi-variable optimization considering time-of-use rates, demand charges, power factor penalties, and renewable generation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much data do we need for predictive maintenance?

For supervised models (predicting known failure modes), we typically need 6-12 months of historical data with at least 5-10 examples of each failure type. For unsupervised anomaly detection, 1-3 months of "normal" operation data is sufficient to establish baselines.

Can you integrate with our existing SCADA or historian?

Yes. We extract data via OPC UA, Modbus, MQTT, SQL database connectors, or file-based ingestion (CSV, Parquet). We've integrated with Wonderware Historian, OSIsoft PI, Ignition, and custom SQL databases.

What hardware is required for edge deployment?

For simple monitoring and alerting, an ESP32-S3 or Raspberry Pi may suffice. For computer vision or complex ML models, we recommend NVIDIA Jetson Orin or Intel NUC with OpenVINO. We specify hardware based on your inference latency, accuracy, and budget requirements.

How do you handle model drift?

All deployed models include automated drift detection. When input data distribution or prediction accuracy degrades beyond thresholds, the system alerts our engineering team and triggers a retraining pipeline using recent data.

Is our data secure?

Absolutely. For on-premise deployments, your data never leaves your facility. For cloud deployments, we use encrypted transmission (TLS 1.3), encrypted storage (AES-256), and network isolation. We can sign NDAs and comply with your data governance policies.
