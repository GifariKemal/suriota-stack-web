INTRODUCTION

Industrial monitoring, built for scale.

Traditional SCADA was built for a single plant. Its assumptions, a fixed I/O list, a known set of operators, a single physical site, break down the moment a customer needs to oversee multiple facilities, a vessel fleet, or a regional operations portfolio. SURGE was built for that second case. It is multi-tenant by design, multi-site by default, and assumes the operator persona may be anyone from a panel-room engineer to a CFO reading a monthly KPI on a phone.

Multi-tenancy goes deeper than a login screen. A single SURGE deployment isolates customers, sites, and roles at the data layer, the API layer, and the dashboard layer. Two customers on the same physical SURGE instance cannot see each other's tags, alerts, or audit trail under any circumstance. Within a customer, roles segment what an operator versus an engineer versus an executive sees. This isolation is what makes SURGE viable as a platform that can carry an enterprise's whole operations portfolio rather than a single plant's instance.

In the Indonesian regulatory context, SURGE intersects three live obligations. KLHK SPARING requires real-time water-quality telemetry from industrial wastewater operations; BKPM permitting increasingly expects auditable operational data; PLN net-metering for PLTS installations requires structured export reporting. Each generates structured data that SURGE captures, normalizes, and reports, turning compliance from a quarterly fire drill into a background hum of the operations layer.

Critically, SURGE does not replace SCADA, DCS, or field-level control. It sits above them. The plant's existing control system continues to do what it does, closed-loop control, safety interlocks, batch sequencing, while SURGE reads from it (or from gateways or directly from sensors) to provide the unified-operations layer above. Customers preserve their existing investment in SCADA while gaining the visibility, multi-site reporting, and compliance automation a traditional SCADA platform was never architected to deliver.
